Security & Trust

TBook Yield Layer is designed with security as a foundational principle. Every layer of the system — from smart contracts to operational processes — is built to protect customer funds.

Multi-Signature Custody​

All vault operations require 2-of-3 multisig approval. No single party can unilaterally move funds or modify vault parameters.

  • Deposit epoch settlement requires multisig authorization
  • Redeem epoch settlement requires multisig authorization
  • Vault configuration changes require multisig authorization

Role Separation​

The vault enforces strict role separation to minimize risk:

RolePermissionsPurpose
AdminUpdate vault configuration, pause/unpauseSystem governance
CuratorFreeze epochs, trigger settlementsDay-to-day operations
OperatorExecute settlement transactionsTransaction execution

No single role has complete control over the vault. Critical operations require coordination between multiple roles.

Third-party Audited​

The TBook Vault smart contracts have been independently audited by ScaleBit/MoveBit, a leading blockchain security firm. The audit covered:

  • Smart contract logic and state management
  • Access control and authorization
  • Fund flow and accounting integrity
  • Edge cases and potential attack vectors

24-Hour Timelock Protection​

Critical vault parameter changes are subject to a 24-hour timelock. This provides a window for stakeholders to review and, if necessary, intervene before changes take effect.

On-chain Verifiable​

All vault state is stored on-chain and publicly verifiable:

  • Total shares outstanding — verify the total supply at any time
  • Vault USDC balance — confirm funds held in the vault
  • Share price history — track NAV updates over time
  • Epoch settlements — audit every deposit and redeem batch
  • User balances — verify individual share holdings

Non-custodial Architecture​

TBook Yield Layer is non-custodial by design:

  • Platforms control their own signing keys — TBook never has access to customer wallets
  • Customer funds flow directly to the vault smart contract — no intermediary custody
  • Redemptions are claimable directly from the vault — no dependency on TBook for withdrawals
  • All transactions require customer or platform authorization — TBook cannot initiate transactions on behalf of customers
