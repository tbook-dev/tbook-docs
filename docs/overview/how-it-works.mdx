# How It Works

## Assets, rails, and vaults

The platform decouples three things that other systems hard-wire together:

- **Assets** — the RWA products themselves. `rcusdp` (USD yield from tokenized T-bill / money-market strategies) and `xaua` (tokenized gold) live natively on Sui.
- **Rails** — the path from where your users' USDC sits to the asset.
- **Vaults** — an asset made investable on a specific rail. One asset can have several vaults.

```
                 ┌──────────────────────  Asset (native on Sui)  ─────────────────────┐
                 │                                                                    │
USDC on Sui ───────────► sui:direct rail ───────────► rcusdp-sui / xaua-sui           │
                 │        (same chain, no bridge)                                     │
                 │                                                                    │
USDC on Solana ────────► solana:gateway rail ───────► rcusdp-sol                      │
                 │        (gateway program + Circle CCTP,                             │
                 │         bridging handled entirely by TBook)                        │
                 └────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

If your users hold USDC on Sui, deposits go **straight into the Sui vault — no bridge, no extra latency**. If they hold USDC on Solana, the gateway rail accepts the deposit on Solana and TBook operates the cross-chain leg invisibly. Your integration code is identical either way; only the `vaultId` and settlement timing differ.

New chains and new assets appear as new vault entries in the [asset catalog](/api-reference/vault-endpoints) — your integration does not change.

## Settlement cycles

Every vault settles in **cycles**. Requests accumulate while a cycle is `accepting`; at the cutoff the cycle closes and moves to `processing` (assets are deployed to, or withdrawn from, the underlying RWA strategy); when results are final the cycle **settles** and balances update.

Cycles exist because real-world assets take real time to subscribe and redeem (T-bills settle T+1; gold subscription via the issuer settles T+3), and because batching makes settlement cheaper and easier to verify on-chain.

You never manage cycles. You observe them:

| Where | What you see |
|---|---|
| `GET /v1/vaults/:vaultId` | `currentCycle: {number, status: accepting \| processing, cutoffAt, expectedSettlementAt}` |
| Positions & transactions | `settlementCycle` on every pending record and intent |
| Webhooks | `vault.cycle.closed` → `vault.cycle.settled` (or `vault.cycle.rolled_back`), plus per-account `transaction.*.settled` events |

### What your user experiences

| Action | Immediately | After the cycle settles |
|---|---|---|
| Deposit | "Processing — will settle T+1" (cancelable) | Shares credited; `transaction.deposit.settled` webhook with exact shares and price |
| Redeem (queued) | "Processing redemption" | USDC claimable; `transaction.redeem.settled` webhook |
| Claim | USDC arrives in the account's own wallet immediately | — |
| Instant redeem (`rcusdp-sui` only) | USDC immediately, at a higher fee | — |

Timing per vault is published in the catalog (`settlement.deposit`, `settlement.redeem`, `settlement.cutoffUtc`) — currently T+1 for rcUSDP vaults and T+3 for `xaua-sui` (cutoff 10:00 UTC).

## The transaction model: build → sign → submit → confirm

1. **Build** — `POST /v1/vaults/:vaultId/tx/deposit` (or redeem/claim/cancel) returns an **unsigned** transaction plus an `intentId`.
2. **Sign & submit** — your wallet infrastructure signs: a WaaS provider for `individual` accounts, your backend key for `omnibus`, a multisig flow for `treasury`/`business`. You submit to the chain RPC.
3. **Confirm** — optionally report the digest back (`POST /v1/transactions/:intentId/confirm`) for instant status tracking; TBook's indexer also detects the transaction on-chain within about a minute either way.
4. **Settle** — the settlement cycle completes and the intent reaches `settled`; webhooks notify you at each step.

This is why the platform is non-custodial: TBook can *propose* transactions but can never execute them.

## Prices and yield

Each asset's `price` is its NAV per share/unit, refreshed from the asset's NAV source and exposed in the catalog and `GET /v1/vaults/:vaultId/price`. For yield assets, `apy` is published alongside. Your users' earnings are computed from indexed history and live positions — see [Positions & Earnings](/api-reference/positions-earnings) for the exact formulas.
